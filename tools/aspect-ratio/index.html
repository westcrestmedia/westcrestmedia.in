Questions

Frequently Asked

What is an aspect ratio?
An aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between a frame's width and height, expressed as two numbers separated by a colon (e.g. 16:9). It defines the shape of the image regardless of its actual pixel size.
What aspect ratio should I use for YouTube?
YouTube recommends 16:9 for standard videos (1920×1080 or 3840×2160 for 4K). For Shorts, use 9:16 (1080×1920). Use the presets above to auto-fill these values instantly.
How do I keep the same ratio when resizing?
Enable the "Lock aspect ratio" toggle, then change either the width or height | the other dimension will update automatically to maintain your ratio. Or use the Scale Calculator for any target size.
What's the difference between 16:9 and 1.78:1?
They're the same ratio expressed differently. 16:9 is the simplified whole-number form, while 1.78:1 (or 1.7778) is the decimal equivalent. This tool shows both | use whichever your software requires.
